    How to Travel Smart and Save Taxes with LTA

    What is Leave Travel Allowance?

    Leave Travel Allowance policy is implemented by many organizations to provide employees with a financial benefit to undertake vacations. Leave Travel Allowance is an allowance that an employee receives when travelling on a leave. However, a lot more goes into claiming these tax benefits.

    Eligibility Criteria for Claiming LTA Exemption

    To claim LTA benefits, you must be a salaried employee and have taken leave to travel within India with your family. Your family includes your spouse, children, dependent parents, and siblings.

    Tax Implications of LTA under the Income Tax Act, 1961

    LTA offers tax relief on your travel expenses. Under Section 10(5) of the Income Tax Act, 1961, you can claim an exemption of actual travel costs incurred or the LTA amount received from your employer, whichever is lower. Remember, LTA is exempt only for two journeys in a block of four calendar years.

    However, please note that the tax exemption of LTA is not available in case you choose new income tax regime.

    Limitations and Restrictions on LTA Exemption

    Documentation Required for Claiming LTA Exemption

    To claim LTA exemption, maintain travel-related bills, tickets, and boarding passes as proof of travel. Ensure that these documents clearly state the travel dates, mode of transportation, and the names of family members traveling with you.
