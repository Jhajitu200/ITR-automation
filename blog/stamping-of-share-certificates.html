    Stamping of Share Certificates

    A practical insight of stamping of shares certificates

    If you are a director or KMP or a consultant of a company having registered office in Gurugram, there are a lot of compliances and regulatory requirements that you should be aware of. One such complex compliance is Stamping of Share Certificates. In today’s time Stamping of Share Certificates in Gurugram seems to be a complex and time taking procedure as it is still done in a completely physical mode and not via any online portal.

    The stamping of Share Certificate is completed after the payment of stamp duty applicable on issue of share certificates. And finally, a Share Allotment Stamp Duty Certificate is issued by the authority.

    Share Certificate Stamping Requirement

    Every Company issuing shares to its investors, shareholders, employees or any other person, is required to issue the respective share certificates within two months of allotment of such shares or in case of a newly incorporated company, within two months from the date of incorporation.

    Further, the share certificate shall be accordingly stamped within 30 days of issue of such share certificates.

    Rate of Stamp Duty

    The rate of stamp duty varies from state to state. In Haryana, the rate of stamp duty on issue of share certificate is Rs. 1/- (Rupees One Only) per Rs. 1,000/- (Rupees One Thousand only) of the value of shares (0.1% of the total amount of shares issued).

    Relevant Authority for Stamping of Share Certificates: In Gurugram, the relevant authority for stamping of share certificates is the Collector of Stamps, Revenue Department, Gurgaon, Haryana.

    Please note that only a Company Secretary or Chartered Accountant (whether in employment or in practice) are authorized to undertake the stamping process in Gurugram, Haryana.

    Non-Compliance

    In case if the company fails to apply for the stamping of share certificates within the stipulated time period, the Authority shall levy a penalty up to 10 times of the stamp duty amount.  

    For example, ABC Ltd. issues share worth Rs. 10 Lakhs. Now as per the prescribed rate the stamp duty payable shall be Rs. 1,000.

    In case if ABC Ltd. delays in submitting the stamping documents with the relevant authority, the total amount payable can go up to Rs. 10,000.

    Documents required

    1. Application form duly affixed with Rs. 10/- court fee stamp.

    2. Certified copy of Share Certificates

    3. Copy of Form PAS-3/ Copy of Spice Form Part B

    4. Certified copy of board resolution 

    5. Certified copy of PAN of company

    6. Certified copy of Memorandum and Articles of Association

    7. Certified copy of list of existing directors

    8. Certified copy of list of Allottees/Share Certificate Holders

    9. Certified Authority letter of Company Secretary/Chartered Accountant (having a valid ICSI/ICAI membership).

    (These documents shall be submitted together in the sequence as provided above)
