    ITR Filing Deadline and Important Dates

    The due date to file Income Tax Return (ITR) in India is July 31st of the year succeeding the financial year for which ITR is to be filed. For FY 22-23, return of income is to be filed on or before 31st July 2023. This annual milestone marks the culmination of a financial year, requiring taxpayers to report their income, deductions, and tax liability to the Income Tax Department. ITR which is filed after 31st July is known as a belated return. A belated return attracts penalties under the Income Tax Act.

    Penalties for Late Filing

    The Income Tax Department enforces penalties to uphold the importance of timely tax compliance and discourage procrastination.

    If you fail to file your ITR on or before the due date, you may face additional interest and penalties under Section 234F of the Income Tax Act, 1961. As per this provision, the penalty for late filing can be as follows:

    A. If your total income does not exceed Rs. 5 lakhs, the penalty for late filing is limited to Rs. 1,000. This means you will be liable to pay a flat penalty of Rs. 1,000 if you file your ITR after 31st July but before 31st March of the assessment year.

    B. For taxpayers whose total income exceeds Rs. 5 lakhs, a higher penalty is levied. If you file your ITR after 31st July but before 31st March of the assessment year, the penalty increases to Rs. 5,000.

    Note: No penalty is levied if your total income is less than Rs. 2,50,000.
