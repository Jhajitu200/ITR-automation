    Evaluate eligibility for ITR

    The first step towards filing ITRs in India is to determine whether you are eligible to file an ITR. The Income Tax Department has laid down certain criteria for taxpayers to file their ITRs.

    • Individuals with an income exceeding the prescribed basic exemption limit are required to file ITRs. The Budget of 2023 has increased the threshold limit for individuals opting for the new tax regime. What this means is, the basic exemption limit in the new tax regime has been increased to INR 3 lakh, which was INR 2.5 lakh earlier.

    • Apart from the above criteria, individuals who earn income from sources other than their salary, such as rent, interest, capital gains, etc., are also required to file ITRs, irrespective of their income level. Additionally, individuals who have earned income from a business or profession or those who have losses to carry forward are also required to file ITRs.

    Documents Required

    Before filing your Income Tax Return (ITR) in India, it is crucial to gather and keep all the required documents ready. This ensures that you file your ITRs accurately and on time. Here is a comprehensive list of documents that you need to keep handy while filing your ITRs -

    • Permanent Account Number (PAN) Card

    • Aadhaar Card

    • Bank Statements

    • Form 16 issued by your employer

    • TDS Certificates

    • Proof of Investments

    • Capital Gains Statements

    • Rent Receipts

    Types of ITR Forms

    It is essential to identify which ITR form is applicable to you based on your sources of income and other factors. Filing the wrong ITR form may result in the rejection of your return, and you may have to re-file your ITR, which can lead to delays and penalties.

    Here are the different types of ITR forms available for taxpayers in India:

    •  ITR-1: This form is for individuals with a total income of up to Rs. 50 lakh, who have income from salaries, one house property, other sources (interest, etc.), and agricultural income up to Rs. 5,000.

    • ITR-2: This form is for individuals and Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs) with a total income of up to Rs. 50 lakh, who have income from salaries, more than one house property, capital gains, other sources (interest, etc.), and agricultural income exceeding Rs. 5,000.

    •  ITR-3: This form is for individuals and HUFs who have income from proprietary businesses or professions.

    •  ITR-4: This form is for individuals, HUFs, and firms with a total income of up to Rs. 50 lakh, who have income from a business or profession computed under the presumptive income scheme.

    •  ITR-5: This form is for partnerships, limited liability partnerships (LLPs), associations of persons (AOPs), and bodies of individuals (BOIs).

    • ITR-6: This form is for companies not claiming exemption under Section 11 of the Income Tax Act.

    • ITR-7: This form is for persons, including companies, who are required to furnish returns under Section 139(4A), Section 139(4B), Section 139(4C), or Section 139(4D) of the Income Tax Act.

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    Filing Income Tax Returns (ITRs) can be a daunting task, and it is common for taxpayers to make mistakes while filing their returns. Here are some of the most common mistakes to avoid:

    • Providing Incorrect Personal Details: Ensure that you enter the correct personal details, such as your name, date of birth, and PAN card number. Any incorrect information may result in the rejection of your ITR.

    • Mismatch of Income and Deductions: One of the most common errors made by taxpayers is the mismatch of income and deductions. Ensure that you accurately report your income from all sources and claim deductions only for the eligible expenses.

    • Missing Out on Exemptions: Taxpayers often forget to claim deductions or exemptions they are eligible for, such as HRA, LTA, or medical expenses. It is essential to keep track of all the exemptions and deductions and claim them while filing your ITR.

    • Not Verifying Your ITR: After filing your ITR, it is important to verify it either electronically or by sending a signed physical copy to the Income Tax Department. Failing to verify your ITR may result in its rejection.

    • Filing the Wrong ITR Form: Filing the wrong ITR form is a common mistake made by taxpayers. Ensure that you download and fill out the correct ITR form based on your sources of income and other factors.
